Bholi Footprints without Feet — Prose

Chapter Summary

Bholi is a heart-touching story written by K.A. Abbas. The story highlights important social issues such as discrimination against girls, child marriage, lack of education for women, and the importance of self-respect. Through the life of a simple village girl named Bholi, the author shows how education can completely change a person's life and personality.

The story begins with the introduction of a little girl named Sulekha, who was lovingly called Bholi by everyone because she was simple, innocent, and slow in understanding things. She was the youngest daughter of Numberdar Ramlal, a wealthy village headman. When Bholi was only ten months old, she fell from her cot and suffered a head injury. After that accident, she was unable to speak properly and started stammering. Later, she also suffered from smallpox, which left deep scars on her face. Because of these physical problems, people considered her unattractive and unintelligent.

Bholi's parents and sisters treated her differently from the other children. They believed that she would never become successful or get married because of her appearance and speech problem. Her sisters often laughed at her, and the villagers made fun of her whenever she tried to speak. As a result, Bholi became quiet, frightened, and lacked confidence. She hardly spoke to anyone and spent most of her time alone.

One day, a primary school was opened in the village. The Tehsildar advised Ramlal to send his daughters to school so that other villagers would also educate their children. Ramlal did not want to send his beautiful daughters because he thought education might reduce their chances of getting married. Finally, he decided to send Bholi to school since he believed she had little chance of marriage anyway.

When Bholi was told that she would be taken to school, she became frightened. She thought that her parents were sending her away because they no longer loved her. However, her mother dressed her in clean clothes for the first time in many years. Although the clothes were old, Bholi felt happy because no one had ever taken so much care of her before.

At school, Bholi met a kind and loving teacher who completely changed her life. Unlike the villagers, the teacher did not laugh at her stammering. She spoke gently and encouraged Bholi to speak slowly without fear. She assured her that with regular practice and education, she would learn to read, write, and speak confidently. For the first time in her life, someone believed in her abilities.

Bholi started attending school every day with great enthusiasm. She worked hard and carefully followed her teacher's advice. Gradually, she learned to read books, write neatly, and speak more confidently. As her education progressed, her fear disappeared, and her confidence increased. She began understanding the difference between right and wrong and developed the courage to express her thoughts.

Many years passed, and Bholi grew into an educated and confident young woman. Although the scars on her face remained, her education had completely transformed her personality. She was no longer the frightened little girl who was afraid to speak. She had become intelligent, independent, and self-respecting.

One day, Ramlal arranged Bholi's marriage with a rich but old man named Bishamber Nath. He was much older than Bholi and already had children from his first wife. Although Ramlal knew about the age difference, he agreed because he believed no one else would marry Bholi.

On the wedding day, Bishamber Nath arrived with the wedding procession. During the marriage ceremony, he noticed the smallpox scars on Bholi's face. Feeling ashamed to marry her, he demanded a dowry of five thousand rupees before completing the marriage. He knew that Ramlal was desperate to get his daughter married and thought he could force him to pay.

Ramlal was shocked and deeply embarrassed. Although paying such a large amount was difficult, he was ready to arrange the money to save his family's honour. He pleaded with Bishamber Nath to accept Bholi without demanding dowry, but the greedy bridegroom refused.

At that moment, Bholi showed the confidence she had gained through education. She clearly understood that Bishamber Nath was insulting her and her family. She courageously refused to marry such a greedy and selfish man. In front of everyone, she declared that she would never marry a person who valued money more than human dignity. Her decision surprised the entire village because nobody expected the once timid Bholi to speak with such courage.

After the wedding procession left without the bride, Ramlal became worried about Bholi's future. However, Bholi comforted her parents. She promised that she would remain with them, look after them in their old age, and continue working as a teacher in the same school where she had received her education. Her teacher smiled proudly because she had successfully transformed Bholi into a confident and independent woman.

The story ends with a powerful message that education not only provides knowledge but also gives people confidence, self-respect, and the courage to fight against social evils. Bholi's transformation from a frightened, neglected girl into a brave and educated woman proves that every child deserves love, respect, and the opportunity to learn.

Theme

The story focuses on the importance of education, women's empowerment, self-respect, and equality. It strongly opposes social evils such as dowry, gender discrimination, child marriage, and judging people by their appearance. The chapter shows that education has the power to transform lives and make people confident enough to stand against injustice.

Message for Students

This chapter teaches us that education is the greatest strength a person can have. It helps us become confident, independent, and capable of making the right decisions. We should never judge people by their appearance or physical disabilities because everyone has unique talents and abilities. The story also reminds us to oppose social evils like dowry and gender discrimination and always respect ourselves and others. Like Bholi, we should have the courage to stand for what is right, even if it means going against society.
