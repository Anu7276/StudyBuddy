The Thief's Story Footprints without Feet — Prose

Chapter Summary

The Thief's Story is a touching and inspiring story written by Ruskin Bond. It is about trust, kindness, honesty, and the power of giving someone a second chance. The story is narrated by a young thief named Hari Singh, who changes his life because of the kindness shown by another person.

Hari Singh is a fifteen-year-old boy who has no home, no family, and no permanent job. He earns money by stealing and cheating people. He frequently changes his name because it helps him escape from the police after committing thefts. Hari believes that stealing is an easy way to survive, and he has never trusted anyone in his life.

One day, Hari meets a young man named Anil at a wrestling match. Anil is about twenty-five years old, simple, kind, and easy-going. Hari realizes that Anil looks generous and decides to become friends with him so that he can steal from him later. Hari introduces himself and asks Anil if he can work for him.

Anil honestly tells Hari that he cannot pay him a salary because he does not earn much money. Instead, he offers Hari food, a place to stay, and promises to teach him how to cook and write. Hari happily agrees because he sees it as a good opportunity. Although he still plans to steal from Anil one day, he starts living with him.

At first, Hari's cooking is terrible. He burns the food and even puts too much salt in the meals. However, Anil does not get angry. Instead, he patiently teaches Hari how to cook properly. Hari also learns small household responsibilities like shopping for vegetables and preparing meals.

Soon, Anil begins teaching Hari how to read and write. Every evening, he helps him learn letters and words. Hari slowly realizes that education can change his future. He understands that if he becomes educated, he can get a respectable job and earn money honestly instead of stealing all his life. This thought creates a conflict in his mind because he is still living as a thief.

One evening, Anil returns home after receiving payment for one of his writing jobs. Hari notices Anil counting six hundred rupees and placing the money under his mattress before going to sleep. Seeing such a large amount of money, Hari is tempted to steal it. Late at night, when Anil is sleeping peacefully, Hari quietly removes the money from under the mattress and leaves the house.

Hari plans to catch the Lucknow Express train and escape to another city. He believes he can spend the stolen money and continue his life as a thief. However, while walking towards the railway station, it starts raining heavily. Hari reaches the station but does not board the train. As he stands there alone, he begins thinking deeply about Anil's kindness.

Hari remembers that Anil never treated him badly. He trusted him completely, gave him shelter, fed him, and most importantly, started teaching him to read and write. Hari realizes that stealing the money would destroy Anil's trust forever. He also understands that six hundred rupees will finish one day, but education and honesty can help him build a better future for the rest of his life.

Feeling guilty and ashamed, Hari decides to return. He quietly goes back to Anil's house in the middle of the night. Carefully, he places the stolen money back under the mattress exactly where it had been. Then he goes to sleep, feeling nervous about what will happen the next morning.

When Hari wakes up, Anil behaves normally. He hands Hari a fifty-rupee note and tells him that from now on he will be paid regularly. Then he says that they will continue learning to write sentences. Hari notices that the notes are still wet from the rain. He immediately understands that Anil knows about the theft and also knows that Hari returned the money.

Instead of scolding or punishing Hari, Anil chooses to forgive him. He understands that Hari has realized his mistake and deserves another chance. His kindness and trust make a deep impact on Hari. For the first time in his life, Hari feels respected and decides to leave behind his dishonest life. He understands that becoming an educated and honest person is much more valuable than becoming a successful thief.

The story ends on a hopeful note. Hari's decision to return the money marks the beginning of a new life based on honesty, trust, and self-respect. Anil's forgiveness proves that kindness and understanding can change a person's heart more effectively than punishment.

Theme

The chapter explores the themes of trust, honesty, kindness, forgiveness, and personal transformation. It shows that education has the power to change a person's life, and that trust can inspire someone to become a better human being. The story also teaches that everyone deserves a second chance if they truly regret their mistakes.

Message for Students

This chapter teaches us that honesty is always better than dishonesty. Money can provide temporary happiness, but knowledge, good character, and trust bring lifelong success. We should never misuse the trust that others place in us. Like Hari Singh, people can change if they realize their mistakes and choose the right path. Like Anil, we should also learn to forgive others and encourage them to improve instead of judging them only by their past actions.
