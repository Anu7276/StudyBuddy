Evans Tries an O-Level Vistas — Prose

Chapter Summary

Evans Tries an O-Level is a thrilling story written by Colin Dexter. It is full of suspense, intelligence, planning, and unexpected twists. The story revolves around a clever prisoner named James Roderick Evans, who successfully escapes from prison despite the strict security arrangements made by the authorities. The chapter highlights the themes of intelligence, overconfidence, careful planning, and the importance of thinking beyond the obvious.

James Roderick Evans, popularly known as Evans the Break, is a skilled criminal who has escaped from prison three times in the past. Because of his repeated escapes, he is considered one of the most dangerous prisoners in England. This time, he is kept in Oxford Prison under strict security. However, Evans wishes to appear for the O-Level German Examination, and the prison authorities decide to allow him to take the exam inside his prison cell.

The Governor of Oxford Prison wants to ensure that Evans does not escape again. Therefore, he personally supervises all the security arrangements. Two prison officers remain inside Evans' cell throughout the examination. The Governor also checks the examination papers, the question paper, and even the identity of the invigilator to prevent any possibility of cheating or escape.

The invigilator arrives at the prison as scheduled. He introduces himself as Reverend Stuart McLeery, a teacher from St. Mary's Merton College. He carries all the necessary examination materials, including the question paper, answer sheets, and a small inflatable cushion because he says he suffers from piles. The Governor carefully examines everything and allows the examination to begin.

As Evans starts writing the examination, everything appears normal. The prison officers carefully watch him while the Governor monitors the situation from his office. Since the examination lasts for some time, everyone believes that the arrangements are perfect and that Evans has no chance of escaping.

Suddenly, the Governor receives a telephone call warning him that there may be an attempt to help Evans escape during the examination. The Governor immediately becomes alert. He rushes towards Evans' cell to check whether everything is safe.

When the Governor reaches the cell, he finds a shocking scene. The invigilator, McLeery, is lying injured on the floor with blood all over him. Evans has disappeared from the cell. The prison officers are confused because they never noticed anything unusual. It appears that Evans has somehow attacked the invigilator and escaped.

The injured McLeery tells the Governor that Evans has stolen his clothes and escaped disguised as the invigilator. He asks the officers to chase Evans quickly before he gets too far. Following McLeery's advice, the police immediately begin searching for Evans.

Using the clues provided by the injured man, the police eventually reach a hotel where they find a man who appears to be Evans. The officers are pleased because they believe they have successfully captured the escaped prisoner. The Governor feels satisfied that Evans has finally been caught after another escape attempt.

However, the story takes an unexpected turn. The man found at the hotel is not Evans but the real Reverend Stuart McLeery. The person who came to the prison pretending to be McLeery was actually one of Evans' friends in disguise. The blood on his body was fake, and the injury had been carefully planned to mislead the prison authorities.

In reality, Evans had changed into different clothes during the examination and escaped according to a well-organized plan prepared by his friends. Every detail of the escape had been planned in advance, including the fake invigilator, the misleading telephone call, and the false clues that sent the police in the wrong direction.

Although Evans is eventually found and arrested once again, the story does not end there. The Governor proudly believes that this time Evans cannot escape because he personally accompanies him in a prison van. However, when the van starts moving, the Governor realizes that the police officers inside are not genuine policemen. They are actually Evans' friends wearing police uniforms.

Evans smiles and calmly thanks the Governor before escaping once again. The fake police officers drive away with Evans, leaving the Governor completely shocked and helpless. Evans successfully escapes for the fourth time, proving that he had planned every step of the operation much more carefully than the prison authorities.

The story ends with a sense of irony. Although the Governor had made excellent security arrangements and believed he had outsmarted Evans, the prisoner proved to be even more intelligent. His careful planning, confidence, and teamwork allowed him to escape despite all the precautions taken against him.

Theme

The chapter explores the themes of intelligence, planning, deception, overconfidence, and the battle of wits between criminals and law enforcement. It shows that success often depends on careful planning and attention to detail. The story also highlights that overconfidence can lead even experienced people to make costly mistakes.

Message for Students

This chapter teaches us that intelligence should always be used for good purposes. Evans was undoubtedly clever, but he used his abilities for crime instead of contributing positively to society. The story also reminds us never to become overconfident, no matter how well prepared we are. Careful observation, teamwork, and thinking beyond the obvious are important in solving problems. True success comes from using our knowledge, honesty, and intelligence in the right direction.
