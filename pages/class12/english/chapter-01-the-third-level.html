CH 1

The Third Level Vistas — Prose

Chapter Summary

The Third Level is a fascinating story written by Jack Finney. It is a blend of reality and fantasy that explores the human desire to escape from the stress, fear, and worries of modern life. The story revolves around a man named Charley, who believes he has discovered a mysterious third level at Grand Central Station in New York. Through this unusual experience, the author shows how people often dream of escaping to a peaceful and happier world when life becomes difficult.

Charley is a thirty-one-year-old man who lives in New York City with his wife, Louisa. Like many people, he has a regular job and leads a normal life. However, the modern world is full of tension, insecurity, competition, and fear, especially because of wars and other problems. Charley often feels tired and mentally disturbed by the fast-moving and stressful life around him.

One evening, while returning home from work, Charley enters Grand Central Station, one of the busiest railway stations in New York. Normally, the station has only two levels. However, Charley accidentally finds himself on what appears to be a mysterious third level. Everything around him looks completely different from the modern world.

On the third level, Charley notices that the station looks old-fashioned, just like it did in the year 1894. The people are dressed in nineteenth-century clothes. Men are wearing long coats, black hats, and handlebar moustaches, while women are dressed in long skirts and elegant dresses. The station is lit by gas lamps instead of electric lights. There are old-style ticket counters, brass spittoons, and steam locomotives instead of modern trains. Even the newspapers sold at the station carry old dates and cost only a few cents.

Charley is amazed by everything he sees. He realizes that he has somehow travelled back in time to the year 1894. He remembers that life during that period was peaceful, simple, and free from the worries of the modern world. There were no world wars, nuclear weapons, or the fast-paced lifestyle that troubles people today.

Charley decides to buy two tickets to Galesburg, Illinois, a small town where life was quiet and comfortable. He wants to take his wife Louisa there and spend the rest of their lives in peace. However, when he offers modern currency to the ticket clerk, the clerk refuses to accept it because the money belongs to a future time. The clerk asks for old-style currency that was used in 1894. Since Charley does not have such money, he is unable to buy the tickets.

After returning to the present, Charley exchanges three hundred dollars of modern currency for old-style currency, hoping to visit the third level again and buy the tickets. He searches Grand Central Station many times but never finds the mysterious third level again. It seems to have disappeared completely.

Charley discusses his strange experience with his friend Sam Weiner, who is a psychiatrist. Sam believes that Charley is simply trying to escape from the pressures of modern life. According to Sam, the third level exists only in Charley's imagination. He explains that people often create imaginary worlds when they become unhappy or stressed.

Although Charley cannot prove that the third level is real, he continues believing that it truly exists. One day, while checking his collection of old stamps, Charley discovers a surprising letter addressed to him. The letter is written by Sam Weiner and is dated 18 July 1894. In the letter, Sam says that he has successfully reached Galesburg through the third level and is enjoying a peaceful, happy life there. He encourages Charley and Louisa to keep searching because the third level really exists.

This letter creates a mystery because Sam had earlier dismissed Charley's story as imagination. Now, it appears that Sam himself found the third level and travelled to the past. The story leaves readers wondering whether the third level is real or simply a symbol of humanity's desire to escape from life's difficulties.

The author does not give a clear answer about the existence of the third level. Instead, he leaves the ending open for readers to interpret. Some people believe that the third level is a real time-travel experience, while others think it represents Charley's imagination and his wish to escape from the stressful modern world.

Theme

The chapter explores the themes of escapism, stress, insecurity, nostalgia, and the conflict between modern life and the peaceful past. It shows that when life becomes difficult, people often dream of escaping to a simpler and happier world. The story also blends fantasy with reality, encouraging readers to think about the importance of peace, happiness, and mental well-being.

Message for Students

This chapter teaches us that modern life often creates stress and anxiety, but we should learn healthy ways to deal with these challenges instead of escaping from reality. It reminds us to value peace, simplicity, and meaningful relationships. The story also encourages us to use our imagination positively while remaining responsible in real life. Finally, it shows that happiness is not found by running away from problems but by facing them with courage and maintaining a balanced life.
